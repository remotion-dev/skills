# Joke Architecture — the construction layer

> **Authored by Fugu Ultra, 2026-06-27.** This is the rigorous *construction* layer that sits under `comedy-craft`'s taste. Taste decides what survives; this raises the floor — instead of 1 usable joke in 5, produce 3–4 worth cutting from. The human taste-holder still makes the final cut. **100% is not the target.** Loaded by `comedy-craft` (Greg Dean, for individual lines) and by `concept-forge` (UCB game + CLoT, for concepts/escalation; see `concept-forge/references/showrunner-gauntlet.md`).

## Voice rules

Graeham's register: dry · deadpan · economical · built on the twist · clean with a little bite · specific to Bay Area / East Palo Alto real estate · never desperate for the laugh.

- **Good line shape:** Normal real-estate sentence. Quiet left turn. Done.
- **Bad line shape:** Big wacky premise, three tags, emoji, apology, rimshot.

The joke should feel like the sentence noticed something before the reader did.

## Safety rules

**Safe targets:** the Bay Area market · Zillow/Redfin/MLS alerts/portals/saved searches · the buying process · the selling process · lending · escrow · disclosure packets · offer deadlines · the agent's own job · the emotional absurdity of real estate.

**Forbidden targets:** who lives somewhere · who *should* live somewhere · race/color/religion/national origin/sex/gender/disability/familial status/age/income class/immigration status or any proxy · a neighborhood's residents as a category · schools as a coded buyer filter · safety/crime/"up-and-coming" coded language · whether a buyer "belongs" somewhere.

**Active listing rule — the house is the client.** Do NOT roast the home, its size, age, condition, style, lot, street, neighborhood, or anything that lowers confidence in the asset. Roast instead: Zillow guessing, buyers refreshing apps, the market acting like a small weather system, paperwork, escrow, the agent's own professional suffering.
- Bad: *"The house is small, but so is everyone's attention span."*
- Better: *"Bay Area buyers have developed a spiritual relationship with square footage. Mostly bargaining."*

# 1. Greg Dean joke structure (individual jokes, captions, hooks, punchlines)

A joke is a controlled misunderstanding. The setup creates one story in the audience's mind; the punchline reveals a second valid story hiding inside the same language.

**The 5 mechanisms:** **1st Story** (the obvious meaning the setup creates) → **Target Assumption** (the hidden assumption that makes the 1st Story work — what the punchline breaks) → **Connector** (one word/phrase/image with two valid interpretations — the hinge; *no hinge, no joke, just a sentence with confidence*) → **Reinterpretation** (the second meaning of the connector) → **2nd Story** (the surprising new story the punchline reveals — it doesn't decorate, it reveals the setup meant something else).

## Build procedure
1. **Pick a topic** — a true EPA/Bay Area observation (homes move fast; buyers refresh Zillow; pre-approval changes the mood; open houses make adults whisper; disclosure packets are long).
2. **Write a setup that creates a clear 1st Story** — sound normal. *"In East Palo Alto, homes can go pending fast."*
3. **Name the Target Assumption explicitly.** *"'Pending' means the MLS status changed."* (Vague assumption → foggy caption.)
4. **Find a Connector** — one word/phrase/image that can honestly mean two things. Useful RE connectors: pending (MLS status / unresolved emotional state) · confidence (statistical / self-esteem) · approved (lender / romantic) · qualified (financially cleared / emotionally prepared) · interest (loan cost / attraction) · close (finish escrow / physically near) · showing (tour / performance) · offer (bid / polite suggestion) · waived (removed from contract / surrendered as a person) · contingency (contract condition / fragile emotional state) · disclosure (legal doc / personal confession) · commitment (purchase / relationship).
5. **Reinterpret the Connector** — the second meaning must be *valid*, not random.
6. **Build the 2nd Story** — a reality where the second meaning is true.
7. **Write the punchline that reveals it** — put the reveal as late as possible; cut anything after the turn.
8. **Safety check** — does it target the house / residents / protected groups / make an active listing look worse? Is the bite on market/process/platform/agent? If not, rebuild.

```
Topic: / Setup: / 1st Story: / Target Assumption: / Connector: / Second meaning: / Reinterpretation: / 2nd Story: / Punchline: / Trimmed final line: / Safety target:
```

## Worked examples
**"Pending":** Setup *"In EPA, homes can go pending fast."* · Connector **pending** (under contract / unresolved, anxious) · Final: ***"East Palo Alto homes can go pending fast. Around here, pending is less a status than a nervous system."*** (bite = market speed + buyer stress)

**"Confidence range":** Setup *"Zillow gives every EPA home a confidence range."* · Connector **confidence** (statistical / self-esteem) · Final: ***"Zillow gives every East Palo Alto home a confidence range. In this market, even the wrong number needs self-esteem."*** (bite = Zillow)

**"Pre-approved":** Setup *"Get pre-approved before you fall in love with the breakfast nook."* · Connector **approved / fall in love** (financing / romance) · Final: ***"Get pre-approved before you fall in love with the breakfast nook. No one needs to get ghosted by a breakfast nook."*** (bite = financing + attachment)

## Debugging
- **Sounds like an observation, not a joke** → missing the **connector**: find the key word, give it a 2nd valid meaning, rewrite the punchline to reveal it.
- **Feels random** → missing a valid **2nd Story**: the punchline must grow from the setup ("didn't expect that, but yes").
- **Too long** → reveal too early: cut setup detail, move the connector near the end, land the reinterpretation on the final word.
- **Mean in the wrong direction** → wrong target: move the bite off the house/residents onto market/paperwork/portal/process/agent.

# 2. UCB "Game of the Scene" + heightening (reels, carousels, multi-beat, campaigns)

A flat 3-beat is three related jokes sitting next to each other. A **game** is one unusual-but-true idea that keeps producing consequences. The engine question is ***"If that is true, what else is true?"*** — never "what's another joke about this topic?"

**Terms:** Base reality (the normal truth) · Unusual-but-true thing (the recognizable odd behavior/pressure) · Game (the repeatable comic rule) · Heightening (escalating the *same* game through increasingly specific consequences — a ladder, not a drawer full of forks).

## Build procedure
1. **List plain truths** (observations before jokes).
2. **Find the one unusual-but-true thing** (the behavior with comic pressure).
3. **State the game as a rule:** *"In this world, [real-estate thing] behaves like [unexpected frame]."*
4. **Establish base reality** (start normal so the audience knows where they are).
5. **Heighten 3× using if/then** — each beat a *consequence* of the game.
6. **Escalate stakes or specificity** (formality / financial / emotional / institutional absurdity) — don't repeat the same move with different nouns.
7. **End on the sharpest consequence** — inevitable and slightly worse. Deadpan, no cartwheel.

```
Real-estate truth: / Unusual-but-true thing: / Game rule: / Base reality: / Beat 1 (if that's true): / Beat 2: / Beat 3: / Final run: / Safety target:
```

## Worked examples (abridged)
**Open house as museum:** game = *"an EPA open house is a museum for adulthood."* → flyer becomes the exhibit plaque → agent becomes the docent → ***"Please exit through underwriting."***

**Zillow as weather app:** game = *"Zillow is the Bay Area weather app for people who refresh listings indoors."* → "price movement advisory in your saved-search area" → "bring layers, a pre-approval letter, and an appraisal gap" → ***"Severe overbid watch in effect until Sunday at 5."***

**Offer process as college admissions:** game = *"buying in EPA is college admissions with a water heater."* → pre-approval = the transcript → waived contingencies = extracurriculars → ***"We regret to inform you the seller chose a stronger applicant."*** (never reference buyer identity, family status, schools, or "fit.")

## Debugging
- **Beats interchangeable** → you wrote a list: make Beat 2 depend on Beat 1, Beat 3 the logical endpoint; raise stakes/specificity.
- **Premise drifts** → you changed the game: restate it in one sentence, delete beats that don't obey it.
- **Feels forced** → the unusual thing isn't true enough: return to observed behavior — recognition first, exaggeration second.

# 3. CLoT — Creative Leap-of-Thought (concept generation)

Structured bisociation: deliberately force two unrelated frames together until they create a *useful surprise*. Not "be random." It's **real-estate truth + unrelated frame = new comic concept** — and it works only when the outside frame reveals something *true* about the real-estate frame.

## Build procedure
1. **Choose the real-estate anchor** (one concrete truth).
2. **Choose an unrelated frame** — dating apps · airport security · software updates · weather reports · museums · college admissions · restaurant menus · courtroom procedure · fitness trackers · board games · tech support · sports broadcasts. (Avoid frames tied to protected classes, tragedy, illness, crime, or neighborhood stereotypes.)
3. **List mechanics from both frames** (don't write jokes yet).
4. **Map the mechanics 1:1.**
5. **Find the comic collision** — the mapping that reveals a true absurdity.
6. **Write the concept line** (compress the premise).
7. **Generate lines from the concept** — Greg Dean for punchlines, UCB for escalation.
8. **Safety check.**

```
Real-estate anchor: / Unrelated frame: / RE mechanics: / Other-frame mechanics: / Mappings: / Comic collision: / Concept line: / Possible Greg Dean connectors: / Possible UCB game: / Finished sample: / Safety target:
```

## Worked concept lines
- **Home search × dating app:** ***"The MLS is a dating app where the profiles are kitchens and the chaperone is your lender. Swipe right all you want. Someone still has to verify your income."***
- **Offer package × airport security:** ***"The Bay Area offer process is airport security for your money. Documents out, no full-size contingencies, and somehow you're still the one apologizing."***
- **Disclosures × software release notes:** ***"Disclosures are release notes for a house. Known issues documented. Patch history included. By opening escrow, you agree to terms you absolutely read."***

## Debugging
- **Idea feels random** → the outside frame doesn't reveal truth: return to the anchor, list real mechanics, pick a frame with *matching mechanics*, not just funny nouns.
- **Concept only makes one joke** → frame too thin: choose a system with multiple parts; map ≥5 mechanics before writing lines.
- **Safety risk** → wrong frame: avoid crime/illness/protected-class/stereotype; move the collision back to process/paperwork/platforms/lending/agent.

# 4. How the engine applies it

- **`concept-forge`** uses **CLoT** for the concept collision and **UCB Game** for escalation. It must output a real game + a 3-rung if/then ladder + a peak line — *not* three unrelated jokes called a beat sheet ("a drawer full of forks").
- **`comedy-craft`** uses **Greg Dean** to construct and repair individual lines. For every candidate, it must be able to name the 1st Story, Target Assumption, Connector, Reinterpretation, 2nd Story, and the reveal. *If it can't name those, the line probably isn't built. It may still be pleasant. So is mulch.*

**Repair pass example** — weak: *"Zillow is often wrong about EPA homes."* → connector **confidence** → ***"Zillow gives every East Palo Alto home a confidence range. In this market, even the wrong number needs self-esteem."***

# Batch pipeline (every generation batch)
1. **Gather inputs** (format, goal, listing status, verified facts, local context, safe + forbidden targets, required CTA). If active listing → apply the active-listing rule.
2. **Generate many concepts** with CLoT (home search × dating app · open house × museum · offer × airport security · disclosures × release notes · Zillow × weather app · escrow × group project · pre-approval × emotional background check · MLS alerts × emergency broadcast · appraisal gap × cover charge · offer deadline × final exam). Keep true/local/safe/multi-beat/process-pointed; kill who-lives-there / mean-to-listing / too-broad / too-goofy / clever-but-useless.
3. **Build escalation** with UCB Game (each beat a consequence of one rule; cut swappable beats).
4. **Build punchlines** with Greg Dean.
5. **Score hard, 0–5** on Truth · Architecture (1st Story/Connector/2nd Story present?) · Twist (late + clean?) · Voice (dry, not thirsty?) · Safety · Target · Usefulness. **5** = keeper · **4** = light trim · **3** = needs punch-up · **2** = soft · **1** = kill · **0** = unsafe/off-brand/Fair-Housing/attacks-the-listing.
6. **Human cut.** Generate many, score hard, the human cuts. *If every line survives, the scoring is too polite.*

---

# Final craft rules
- The joke must be **true** before it is funny.
- The punchline must **turn**, not decorate.
- The **connector** must support both meanings.
- **Heightening** escalates one game, not three cousins.
- **CLoT** must reveal truth, not randomness.
- For active listings, the house is **never** the target.
- Fair Housing safety is not optional.
- Local texture beats generic "real estate is crazy" jokes.
- Cut until the twist is doing the work.
- **Generate many. Score hard. The human cuts. The architecture raises the floor; taste owns the ceiling.**
