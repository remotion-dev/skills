# Humor Reference — Graeham's Taste Corpus

The highest-leverage file in the humor system. The model imitates concrete examples far better than abstract rules, so this file — Graeham's actual taste, plus the specific bits he flags as landing — is what turns "average" into "sounds like him." Read it before writing any creative/short-form piece.

Two layers:
1. **The roster + profile** (below) — seeded from Graeham's stated favorites. Already usable.
2. **Flagged bits** (the PASTE HERE section) — fills in as Graeham watches and reports back. Gets sharper over time.

---

## The through-line (read this first)

Graeham likes **dry, deadpan, economical, surprising** comedy built on **the twist**. He does NOT gravitate to warm/relatable or physical/animated. Write him sharp, not folksy. Full analysis in `../SKILL.md` → "Graeham's calibrated taste." Edge level: **clean with a little bite** — sharp, never mean, never at a client's expense.

| **Bo Burnham** | Meta, self-aware, smart construction | Acknowledge the format; comment on the thing while doing the thing |
| **Tig Notaro** | Minimalist deadpan, the pause as a tool | Silence/understatement; trust the audience to get there |
| **John Mulaney** | Tight storytelling, callbacks, the button | Structured story with a payoff that returns later |
| **Milton Jones** | Surreal pun-density done *well*, rapid one-liners | Wordplay only when it's genuinely surprising, never groaner |
| **Morgan Murphy** | Dry, dark deadpan one-liners (structure) | Economy + flat delivery on a sharp observation |

**The pattern across all of them:** deadpan delivery + one-liner economy + the twist + a surreal/self-aware streak. Dark where Graeham personally watches; clean-with-a-little-bite where it ships.

### By comedian (mechanism → verbatim source bit → clean transfer)

**Mitch Hedberg** — surreal language audit
- *"I bought a two-bedroom house. But it's up to me how many bedrooms there are… this bedroom is a.k.a. a hallway."* → "Zillow calls it a two-bedroom. But a bedroom is just a room you've decided to sleep in, so the price per bedroom is whatever I want it to be." (trim for posting)
- *"This shirt is dry-clean only, which means it's dirty."* → **"The listing says 'low maintenance,' which means nobody has maintained it."** ★

**Steven Wright** — deadpan surreal logic
- *"Every morning I make instant coffee… so I'll have enough energy to make the regular coffee."* → **"Every morning I check the Zestimate, just to have the energy to go find out what the house is actually worth."** ★
- *"A combination one-way round-trip ticket. You leave Monday, they bring you back the previous Friday."* → "You buy on Monday, we close the previous Friday — before the price went up. That way you already have the equity."

**Anthony Jeselnik** — misdirection (structure only)
- *"Turns out I was right. I was wrong."* → "I told the seller their Zestimate was low and we'd beat it. Turns out I was right. I was wrong. It went for even more than that."
- *"the only time I cried in public was at my mom's funeral… that doesn't mean I'm not also one hell of an actor."* → "People say I get emotional at closings. I do. It's how they know it's finally done. And I'm a very good actor."

**Jimmy Carr** — compression + stat deflation (structure only)
- *"you're ten times more likely to get mugged in London than New York… because you don't live in New York."* → "You're ten times more likely to win a bidding war in Tracy than in Palo Alto. Because you don't live in Palo Alto." (needs the right audience)
- *"someone went, I thought you'd be younger. I said I was."* → "A buyer told me he thought the listing would still be available. I said it was."

**Dan Mintz** — deadpan twist
- *"I got one of those treadmill desks so I can not do work and not exercise at the same time."* → **"I got one of those home-value apps so I can not sell my house and not know what it's worth at the same time."** ★
- *"as a kid: please be the water heater, not a monster. As an adult: please be a monster, not my water heater."* → "As a buyer: please be the wind, not the foundation. As the owner: please be the foundation, not the foundation whose warranty just expired."

**Morgan Murphy** — flat over-confident bad advice
- *"never look at your credit card statement… it is a mirror up to everything horrific about you."* → "Never read your own Zestimate history. It's a mirror up to every decision you almost made and didn't."
- *(the tag)* "How to Lose a Guy in 16 Seconds. And yes, I just gave away how long it takes me to…" → "How to Sell Your House in 9 Days. And yes, I just told you how long mine sat before I dropped the price."

**Bo Burnham** — self-aware / name-the-device
- *"This is a poem by a dog. Roses are gray, violets are a different shade of gray…"* → "Here's a Zestimate's love poem: your home is worth a number. Also a different number. Let's revise it Tuesday."
- *"…Because this is a metaphor for racism!"* (names its own device) → "Rates went up, you waited. Rates went down, you waited. Why are you still waiting? Because this is a metaphor for the part where you missed the bottom."

**Tig Notaro** — minimalist deadpan / literal audit
- *"…they include the question, can you believe it? …I don't know. What is she, about five? Yeah, I can believe that."* → "I love a 'once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.' Is it, though. There's another one next door. So at minimum it's a thrice-in-a-Tuesday opportunity."
- *"all of these questions feel very Google-able."* → "Buyers send me long anxious emails asking what their house is worth. What I do know is the Zestimate already gave them three answers before lunch."

**John Mulaney** — specificity + literalism
- *"that's like saying, I'll transport this convict across state lines, you hold a potato."* → "Zillow says it valued my listing 'using comparable sales.' That's like pricing a Picasso by weighing it next to a ham."
- *(logic audit)* "you only say the word you're worried about" → **"'Priced to sell' is admitting the others aren't. You only label the thing you're worried about — that's the tell."** ★

**Milton Jones** — one-liner economy + absurd counterexample
- *"CIA, KGB, MI5 — they've all got a lot of secrets. KFC, just the one."* → **"Zillow, Redfin, the county assessor — they've all got an estimate of your home's value. The buyer at your door has just the one."** ★
- *"a tiny fishing village in Derbyshire… It's not near the sea."* → "Don't talk to me about a hot market. I just listed a 'rare lakefront lot.' There's no lake. There's a drainage easement that fills up twice a year."

**Ryan Reynolds** — self-aware marketing (the north star)
- *"I call this drink the misdirect. You'll never see it coming."* → "I call this the off-market listing. You'll never see it coming — by the time it hits Zillow, it's already sold." (tagline more than joke)
- *"…just like the aviation gin you love, but with more ounces."* → "Same house you toured last spring, just with a new price. Same square footage, fewer dollars."
- *"fourth-grade geography… and revisiting your own middle-school traumas."* → **"Buying a home teaches you: how a mortgage works, what escrow is, and exactly how much your relationship can take."** ★

**Mark Normand** — compression + language audit
- *"the word 'riddled' — never use it for anything positive… never 'this ice cream's riddled with sprinkles.'"* → "'Cozy' never means big. Nobody writes 'cozy 4,000 square feet.'"
- *"you can tell being an adult is hard by the abbreviations… DUI, DMV, IRS…"* → "You can tell you've become a homeowner by your acronyms. It used to be ETA and RSVP. Now it's the HOA, the APR, the PMI, and the ARM about to adjust."
