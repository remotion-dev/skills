# Bi-Monthly Angle Bank — Bay Area + EPA Market Updates

Six rotation angles for the bi-monthly market update format. Each update picks the next unused angle from this bank. No angle may repeat within 3 consecutive bi-monthly updates.

Full production details for each angle below. The summary table is in `README.md`.

---

## B1 — Buyer Behavior

**Core question:** What separates buyers who win offers from buyers who keep losing?
**Why it works bi-monthly:** Buyer behavior shifts with rate environment, inventory levels, and seasonality — the specific answer changes even when the underlying market is stable.

**Bay Area frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a market stat that implies competition (sale-to-list ratio, cash buyer share, offer count data)
- What the video teaches: The 3 preparation moves that winning buyers make before writing an offer
- Script arc: Stat hook → What the market looks like right now → What winning buyers do → What losing buyers skip → CTA
- GHL keyword: MARKET (send buyer strategy guide)

**EPA frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a supply number (low inventory) or a price-band observation (entry tier still seeing multiple offers)
- What the video teaches: How to position an offer specifically in the EPA price tier that's most competitive right now
- GHL keyword: BUY (send active + off-market EPA listings)

**Imagination prompts for future uses of B1:**
- Next time: Lead with a losing buyer story (anonymous composite) — what went wrong and why
- Next time: Lead with the rate sensitivity angle — how one rate move shrinks the qualified buyer pool
- Next time: Lead with a counter-intuitive stat (a slower market still has multiple offers in one price band)

**Visual direction:** Action-oriented. Couple reviewing documents, agent at kitchen table, split-screen before/after offer scenario.

## B2 — Seller Timing + Pricing Strategy

**Core question:** When is the right time to list, and what's the pricing approach that generates the best outcome right now?
**Why it works bi-monthly:** Optimal list timing shifts seasonally. The penalty for overpricing changes with market temperature. This angle has genuinely different answers in June vs August vs October.

**Bay Area frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a pricing penalty stat (homes overpriced by X% sit Y% longer) or a timing window observation (first 10 days = 80% of buyer interest)
- What the video teaches: The current seasonal window, the pricing strategy that generates competition vs. the one that generates price reductions
- Script arc: Timing window stat → Why the first 10 days are everything → How overpricing burns the listing → The right pricing approach for right now → CTA
- GHL keyword: SELL (send the seller's timing guide)

**EPA frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a time-on-market comparison by pricing strategy in EPA specifically
- What the video teaches: The 3% rule in EPA — homes priced within 3% of comp close in 32 days; everything else averages 66 days
- GHL keyword: READY (send the pre-listing checklist)

**Imagination prompts for future uses of B2:**
- Next time: Lead with a specific price reduction story (what happens to a listing when it sits past day 21)
- Next time: Lead with the best week of the year to list (data-driven, specific to current year)
- Next time: Lead with the "stale listing" rescue — how to relaunch a listing that sat

**Visual direction:** Home staging, for-sale signs, agent walking through a prepared home, before/after listing presentation.

## B3 — Rate Sensitivity Analysis

**Core question:** How does the current rate environment actually affect buyer purchasing power at Peninsula price points — and what does that mean for sellers?
**Why it works bi-monthly:** Rate environment changes constantly. Every 0.25pt move has specific dollar implications at Bay Area price points. This angle can always be re-run with fresh rate data.

**Bay Area frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a specific dollar impact ("At today's 6.9% rate, a buyer approved for $2M in 2021 qualifies for $1.4M today")
- What the video teaches: The qualified buyer pool at each Peninsula price tier at current rates; which tiers have the most competition; which tiers have lost buyers
- Script arc: Rate hook → Dollar impact at multiple price tiers → Which buyers are still active → What sellers at each tier should expect → CTA
- GHL keyword: NUMBERS (send the rate + payment calculator for Peninsula price tiers)

**EPA frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with the EPA-specific rate impact — what percentage of EPA's traditional buyer pool has been squeezed out at current rates
- What the video teaches: The price tier in EPA where rate sensitivity is lowest (often the entry tier with smaller loan amounts or FHA-eligible buyers)
- GHL keyword: COSTS (send the EPA affordability breakdown)

**Imagination prompts for future uses of B3:**
- Next time: Lead with the refinance watch — when rates drop X%, how many current homeowners refinance vs. how many list?
- Next time: Lead with the adjustable vs. fixed comparison for buyers at Peninsula price points
- Next time: Lead with rate lock-in as a social phenomenon — why holders at 3% mortgages aren't selling

**Visual direction:** Numbers/data heavy. On-screen calculators, text overlays with specific payment amounts, comparison tables. Screen-record of mortgage calculator optional.

## B4 — Equity + Wealth Position

**Core question:** For Peninsula homeowners who've held for 5–15 years — where does their equity actually stand, and what are their real options?
**Why it works bi-monthly:** Home values move, rates move, and the refinance vs. sell vs. hold math changes. This angle speaks directly to long-term homeowners who aren't active buyers or sellers but are thinking about their next move.

**Bay Area frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with an equity stat ("The average Peninsula homeowner who bought in 2015 is sitting on $X in equity today")
- What the video teaches: The real options for a long-term Peninsula owner — refinance, HELOC, sell, or hold; the tax implications of each; when each makes sense
- Script arc: Equity hook → What $X in equity actually means in terms of options → Refinance math → Sell + 1031 math → Hold + HELOC math → The decision framework → CTA
- GHL keyword: EQUITY (send the equity analysis request form)

**EPA frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with the EPA-specific long-term owner story (67% of 2026 sellers held 10+ years; their cost basis vs. today's value)
- What the video teaches: The Prop 13 + capital gains calculation for EPA's long-term owners; when selling makes sense despite the tax hit
- GHL keyword: OPTIONS (send the rent-vs-sell analysis tool)

**Imagination prompts for future uses of B4:**
- Next time: Lead with the 1031 exchange explainer — specifically for EPA owners moving to another California investment property
- Next time: Lead with the HELOC vs. cash-out refi comparison at current rates
- Next time: Lead with a specific "what if I'd bought in 2012" calculation — emotional, concrete, relevant

**Visual direction:** Clean, financial-feeling. Calculators, equity growth charts (visual, not spreadsheet-dense), homeowner looking at documents, lifestyle imagery of financial security.

## B5 — Supply Constraint + Inventory Story

**Core question:** Why is inventory so low — and what would it take to change that?
**Why it works bi-monthly:** Inventory levels fluctuate. The structural causes (Prop 13, zoning, rate lock-in) are persistent but the story about them changes as new data, policy changes, or market events provide fresh hooks.

**Bay Area frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a months-of-supply stat that puts inventory in context ("There are X months of supply in San Mateo County. A balanced market has 6. We're at X.")
- What the video teaches: Why inventory is structurally low, what would need to change for it to normalize, and what it means for buyers right now
- Script arc: Inventory stat → What "months of supply" actually means → Why it's this low → What doesn't fix it + what does → Buyer/seller implications → CTA
- GHL keyword: VALUE (send the market supply report)

**EPA frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a new-listing count that's surprisingly low (14 listings in May, as a recent example)
- What the video teaches: The specific EPA supply constraint story — long-term owner lock-in, Prop 13, zoning for single-family; why EPA won't see a flood of inventory soon
- GHL keyword: EPA (send the EPA market intelligence brief)

**Imagination prompts for future uses of B5:**
- Next time: Lead with new construction in the area — what's being built, where, at what price point, and whether it will actually relieve pressure
- Next time: Lead with zoning reform (ADU rules, SB 9 implications) and whether it's moving the needle
- Next time: Lead with a specific policy change (new state housing law, local upzoning vote) and its practical impact

**Visual direction:** Maps, neighborhood aerials, inventory charts (simple bar charts work well), construction footage if available.

## B6 — Seasonal Inflection

**Core question:** What does this specific season typically mean for Bay Area real estate — and where is 2026 deviating from the historical pattern?
**Why it works bi-monthly:** Seasons are predictable in concept but the DEVIATION from the pattern is always fresh. The angle is: "Here's what usually happens in [season], here's what's actually happening, and here's what that means for you."

**Bay Area frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a deviation stat ("Spring is supposed to be the hottest season — but sale-to-list in April was actually lower than January. Here's why.")
- What the video teaches: The Bay Area seasonal pattern (spring peak, summer steady, fall mini-peak, winter slow) + where 2026 is on or off that pattern + what the deviation means
- Script arc: Season expectation → Actual data → What's driving the deviation → Buyer/seller action for this specific moment → CTA
- GHL keyword: MARKET (send the seasonal calendar guide)

**EPA frame:**
- Hook direction: Lead with a season-specific EPA observation — when EPA's peak listing months actually are vs. county, and whether this year is tracking that pattern
- What the video teaches: The EPA-specific seasonal window that buyers and sellers often miss
- GHL keyword: WATCH (send the EPA seasonal timing guide)

**Imagination prompts for future uses of B6:**
- Next time: Lead with a historical comparison ("In 2019, spring looked exactly like this — here's what happened in Q3")
- Next time: Lead with a rate-driven seasonal shift ("Rate moves in Q1 pulled spring buying forward — here's the data")
- Next time: Lead with the winter opportunity frame (contrarian seasonal play for buyers)

**Visual direction:** Seasonal imagery — spring blooms in neighborhoods, summer open houses, fall light, winter quiet streets. Time-lapse of market activity. Seasonal trend charts.
