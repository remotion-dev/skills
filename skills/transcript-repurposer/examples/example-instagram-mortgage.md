# Worked Example — Instagram Mortgage Rate Video

End-to-end example showing how this skill processes a typical SurfFast-downloaded Instagram transcript into a Graeham-voiced content package.


## Phase 2 — Source Brief

```
1. Core Claim: Buyers shouldn't wait for mortgage rates to drop because rates may stay high indefinitely.
2. Hook Strategy: Generic opener ("Hey guys welcome back so today we're talking about mortgage rates") — WEAK. Major upgrade opportunity.
3. Evidence Quality:
   - "rates right now are crazy 7%" → Vague (no date)
   - "few years ago they were 3%" → Vague (no date)
   - "rates might not come down anytime soon" → Opinion (no source)
4. Structure: Hook → Setup → Recommendation → CTA (standard short-form)
5. Target Audience: General buyers, broad consumer
6. Length and Pace: 110 words / ~45s / medium density (~2 claims per 30s)
7. Localization Need: Real estate universal — strong opportunity to localize to Peninsula
```

## Phase 3 — Repurpose Angle

```
Repurpose Angle: Same Claim, Better Evidence + Local Lens hybrid —
the core takeaway (don't wait on rates) is right, but the evidence is
vague and there's no Bay Area context where buyers feel the rate pain
most acutely.
```

## Phase 4 — Research Pack

```
## Research Pack — As of April 2026

Verified Facts (cite-ready):
1. As of April 2026, the 30-year fixed mortgage rate is averaging 6.78% (Freddie Mac PMMS, April 10 2026 release).
2. The Federal Reserve has held the federal funds rate at 4.25-4.50% through Q1 2026, with futures markets pricing zero cuts before Q3.
3. The 2021 rate low of 2.65% was an anomaly driven by COVID-era monetary policy; the 50-year average is ~7.7%.
4. In Peninsula markets (San Mateo County), median 3BR SFH price as of April 2026 is $1.92M (MLS, April 1-15 2026). At 6.78%, a 20%-down buyer faces a $9,950/mo principal + interest payment.
5. The same buyer at 4% (a hypothetical "wait for rates" scenario) would pay $7,330/mo — savings of $2,620/mo, but the Peninsula price typically rises faster than the rate savings during low-rate environments (2020-2022 saw 38% price appreciation locally).

Source-Transcript Claims Filtered Out:
- "rates are like 7%" — Slightly stale, swapped to verified 6.78%
- "few years ago they were 3%" — Vague; replaced with specific 2021 2.65% low + 50-year average context

Bay Area Lens Insertion Points:
- After hook, land the Peninsula median + monthly payment math
- In body, the 38% appreciation stat is the kill-shot for the "wait for rates" plan

Lead Magnet / CTA Recommendation:
- GHL Keyword: OPTIONS
- Lead Magnet: Loan Options Comparison PDF
- Rationale: Buyer-funnel topic, financing-flavored, OPTIONS matches the keyword set
```
