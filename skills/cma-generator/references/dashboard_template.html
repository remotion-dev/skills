Where the Market Is. And What That Means for You

You bought 1030 Bradley Way on April 4, 2022 for $925,000. That was effectively the top of the recent EPA cycle. The market peaked in mid-2022 at around $1.18M median for the broader SFR cohort, then cooled materially into 2023 as the Fed pushed rates from near-zero to over 5%. The 30-year fixed mortgage went from sub-3% in early 2022 to over 7% by late 2023, and the EPA market reset accordingly. Over the four years you have owned this home, prices for similar homes have traded roughly sideways.

Where rates sit today: 30-year fixed clusters between 6.5% and 6.7% across four sources today (Mortgage News Daily, Bankrate California, Realtor.com EPA, Zillow Home Loans). Down from the 2023 peaks above 7%, but still well above the 2020-21 lows that drove the EPA peak when you bought. The retail buyer pool that pays $1M+ for an 810 sqft cottage in EPA is much thinner at 6.6% than at 3.0%. There is no clear catalyst on the horizon to bring rates back to that range. The Fed has paused, inflation is sticky around 2.5 to 3%, and most forecasts suggest rates stay in the 5.5 to 6.5% range through 2027.

What that means for your home: the EPA small-home market is likely to trade in a similar sideways-to-modestly-up pattern for the next 2 to 4 years. Modest annual appreciation (2 to 4 percent) is the base case. A real rebound that pushes you well past your $925K basis probably needs rates back below 5%, and that is not the base case.

The 800 to 1,100 sqft EPA cohort over the last six months shows a sold median of $977,500 and a sold range of $790K to $1,325K. The cohort skews larger and 3-bed compared to your home, and includes one west-of-101 outlier that should not anchor your expectations. When you filter to the right comps (2-bed, 1-bath, under 1,050 sqft, east of 101), the realistic clearing band is $850K to $925K.

What that translates to in cash:

  • At a sale of $895K (the middle of the realistic band) with standard closing costs of roughly 5.5%, your net to you is approximately $846,000, about $79K below your $925K basis on paper.
  • At the top of the realistic band, $925K sale, same friction: net is roughly $874,000, about $51K below basis on paper.

That looks like a loss in isolation. It is not the full picture. Principal paydown over 4 years on a 2022 mortgage is probably $30K to $55K in your favor, depending on your loan structure. That principal lives in your equity, not on the closing statement, but it is real money in your pocket at sale. And if you have lived at 1030 Bradley as your primary residence for at least 2 of the last 5 years, the IRS Section 121 exclusion makes the first $250K of gain tax-free if single, $500K if married. For a property in your range, that means any gain is fully tax-free.

The cleanest read: at a likely sale of $895K to $925K, your real cash-out position is somewhere between $24K below break-even and $4K above break-even, depending on your loan structure and exact residency status. Effectively a wash. Not a win, not a loss. The decision is then: what do you want that ~$845K of cash to do for you over the next several years?

Three Paths Forward. The Real Trade-Off

The question is not just "what does it sell for." It is "what do you want your capital doing for the next several years." Three honest paths:

Path A. Sell now, take the cash, redeploy elsewhere (recommended if your goal is to move on)

Realistic sale price range: $870,000 to $910,000. Standard listing, standard timeline, no special prep or staging. Aim for a 30 to 45 day sale and move on cleanly.

Why this path makes sense if you want out: the EPA small-home market is likely to trade sideways to modestly up for the next 2 to 4 years at minimum. There is no rate-cut catalyst on the horizon strong enough to materially lift your home value inside 24 months. A diversified portfolio (stocks, dividend ETFs, high-yield treasuries) earning 5 to 7 percent over 2 to 3 years likely outpaces what 1030 Bradley does over the same window. If your goal is to put your capital somewhere it works harder than this property, this is the cleanest outcome.

Path B. Optimize for the top of the range. Targeted prep and market timing.

Realistic sale price range: $910,000 to $940,000. Same eventual exit as Path A, but aimed at the top of what this market will pay for an 810 sqft 2/1 east-of-101 cottage. Two levers:

1. Targeted pre-list prep ($5,000 to $10,000): fresh neutral paint throughout, professional deep clean, light staging in living room and main bedroom, landscaping refresh on the front yard. Small dollar amount, meaningful impact on photos and showings for a vacant home.

2. Time the list to the spring market (February to April 2027): retail buyer demand peaks in spring on the Peninsula, especially for first-time-buyer-friendly cottages in EPA. Listing into a strong-demand window typically adds 2 to 4 percent over the same listing in an off-season month.

Trade-off: a 60 to 120 day delay from where you are today, plus the prep budget. Upside is roughly $20K to $40K more on the sale price, which is the difference between "small paper loss" and "real break-even or slight gain" on your basis.

Path C. Hold and rent. For a long-term real estate investor only.

This path makes sense for one specific profile: someone who explicitly wants EPA real estate as a long-term hold piece of their portfolio. Not as a wait-and-see play.

Numbers if pursued: market rent for an 810 sqft 2/1 in this neighborhood runs roughly $2,400 to $2,800 per month. If your original loan locked early 2022 at sub-4%, your principal+interest is probably $3,000 to $3,800 per month, so rent covers most of debt service. Over a 5+ year hold, modest 2 to 4 percent annual appreciation plus continued principal paydown plus mortgage interest deductibility makes this a defensible long-term piece of a real estate portfolio.

Where this path falls short: if your goal is to deploy capital where it earns the most over 2 to 4 years, this is unlikely to be it. Sideways EPA pricing plus the cost of being a landlord (vacancy risk, repairs, property management, tenant turnover) eats into the return. Path C only wins if you specifically want real estate exposure and a multi-year hold.

Notes & Caveats

About the AVM versus the comps

The CoreLogic AVM mid of $993,000 came up materially higher than what the actual sold comps support ($850K-$925K realistic range). The AVM confidence score was 54/100, low. AVMs lean on broader cohort averages and don't always weight the relevant comp set correctly. The comps are the ground truth here. If we go to market based on the AVM, we overprice and chase the market down. The CMA range is the right anchor.

Capital gains exclusion. Worth verifying.

If you lived at 1030 Bradley as your primary residence for at least 2 of the last 5 years, IRS Section 121 lets you exclude up to $250,000 of gain (single) or $500,000 (married filing jointly) from federal capital gains tax. Your tax record currently shows owner-occupied, which is a positive signal. If you qualify, any cash-out gain from this sale is tax-free. Confirm with your CPA before deciding, since this can meaningfully change your real cash position at closing.

Loan structure matters for the rent-and-hold math

The exact monthly carrying cost depends on your original loan amount, rate, and term, which I do not have. Before committing to Path C (hold and rent), pull your loan statement and run a real cash-flow scenario against the expected rent range of $2,400 to $2,800 per month. If your 2022 rate was sub-4%, the math likely supports the hold. If you refinanced or took a higher-rate product, it may not.
