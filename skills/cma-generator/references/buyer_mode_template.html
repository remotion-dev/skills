Read the Listing Carefully. This Is a Structured Bid
This is not a normal listing. The seller is a nonprofit (HIP Housing) running a controlled offer process. The list price is intentionally low to drive competitive bidding. You will be one of multiple offers.

Critical process details from the listing agent's instructions:

  • No preemptive offers. The agent has explicit instructions not to present anything before the offer date. Do not try to jump the line, it will be ignored or returned.
  • Showings begin Friday, May 29 at 1pm. Schedule through Aligned Showings. Plan to see it in person.
  • Multiple weekends of open houses. Expect a lot of foot traffic, which the seller is using to generate competition.
  • Disclosure package posted Saturday, May 30. Review carefully. The home is older (1949), has been used as a shared housing program, and you will want to know about any deferred items.
  • Offer date: Wednesday, June 3. All offers due by the deadline. The seller will likely review on June 4 or 5.

What this means for your offer strategy: you have about a week to prepare a strong, clean, competitive offer. The list at $1,100,000 should not anchor your thinking, it is a starting line, not a finish line. Realistic clearing price based on the comp set is materially higher.

One bonus angle worth using: the seller is a mission-driven nonprofit. A short personal letter explaining your intended use (owner-occupant, family home, community-aligned values) may matter to this seller in ways that would not matter to a typical retail seller. Combined with a strong financial offer, it can move you to the top of a tight pack.

Where the Market Is. Belle Haven Right Now

Belle Haven (the east-of-101 portion of Menlo Park) has been the fastest-clearing entry-level submarket on the Peninsula over the last 6 months. The 900 to 1,500 sqft SFR cohort cleared median $1,230,000 with median days-on-market of 7. The two most relevant recent sales (1208 Henderson at $1,244K, 0 DOM; 1117 Madera at $1,230K, 7 DOM) were both smaller than 1430 Chilco (1,070 and 1,040 sqft vs Chilco's 1,230 sqft).

That tells you two things. First, well-priced Belle Haven homes clear in days, not months, often pre-emptively or with multiple offers. Second, Chilco's $1,100,000 list is well below what comparable homes have actually traded at, which is consistent with a seller's strategy of underpricing to drive competition. Expect the winning bid to land in the $1,200,000 to $1,350,000 range, with the possibility of $1.35M+ if the bidding gets aggressive.

One outlier in the cohort to be aware of: 1268 Carlton sold at $950,000, well below the rest. That sale was likely either a fixer or an off-market transaction at a discount. It is not a useful anchor for what Chilco will sell at.

Three Paths Forward. Offer Strategy

You said your goal is to win this property with a strong offer. Three paths, ranked by aggressiveness:

Path A. Anchor offer with strong terms. Compete on terms more than price.

Offer range: $1,150,000 to $1,200,000. Above list, but only modestly. Win by being the cleanest, fastest, most certain offer in the stack. Levers: pre-underwritten financing or all cash, 21-day close, 3% earnest money deposit, short or waived inspection contingency (after reviewing the disclosure package May 30). Personal letter aligned with HIP Housing's mission.

Risk: in a multiple-offer scenario with a tease list price, $1.15M-$1.20M may not clear. The Henderson and Madera sales went $1.23M-$1.24M for smaller homes. This range bets the seller values terms over headline price, which a nonprofit might or might not.

Path B. Competitive market-aligned offer. Most likely to win without overpaying. (recommended)

Offer range: $1,225,000 to $1,300,000. Right in the cohort's actual clearing band. The Henderson sale at $1.244M for a smaller home is your anchor. At 1,230 sqft (15% bigger than Henderson) with 4 bedrooms (one more than Henderson), Chilco arguably warrants a modest premium. Add strong terms (21-day close, large EMD, waived or short inspection contingency post-disclosure-review) and you give yourself the best probability of winning at a defensible price.

This is the recommendation. It clears the comp set, beats the likely majority of competing offers, and does not require stretching beyond the data.

Path C. Stretch offer. Maximum certainty of winning, willing to pay top of range.

Offer range: $1,325,000 to $1,400,000. All-in to lock down the property. Adds an appraisal gap (you cover any shortfall up to a defined cap), waives most contingencies post-disclosure, fastest possible close. This wins almost certainly, but you may be paying $50K to $100K more than the second-best offer.

Risk: this range exceeds the comp set's recent sold band. If the appraisal comes in low (likely at this price for this property), you cover the gap out of pocket. Only choose this if you are absolutely committed to this property and can absorb the gap without stress.

Notes & Caveats

The 1268 Carlton outlier

1268 Carlton Avenue closed at $950,000, well below the rest of the cohort. We do not have full price-history for that sale, and the $/sqft of $754 suggests either significant deferred maintenance, a structural issue, or an off-market discount. Do not use it as a comparable when shaping your offer. The relevant comps are Henderson at $1,244K and Madera at $1,230K.

Appraisal risk

If you go above ~$1.25M, expect appraisal risk. Appraisers typically anchor on recent closed sales, and the $1.23M to $1.24M cluster in the cohort may pull the appraisal toward that band. Plan for an appraisal gap of $50K to $100K depending on your final offer price. Discuss the gap structure with your lender before submitting.

The HIP Housing angle

The seller, Human Investment Project Inc, is a Bay Area nonprofit that operates shared housing and home-sharing programs. They listed this home after winding down the shared housing use at this address. They likely value: a mission-aligned buyer, a clean and certain transaction, and a price that reflects the home's market value (they cannot underprice a charitable asset). A short personal letter to the board (not to the agent) explaining your story and intended use may meaningfully strengthen your offer.
