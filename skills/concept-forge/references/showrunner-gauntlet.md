# The Showrunner Gauntlet — concept-forge's selection process

**Authored from the Fugu Ultra deep-dive, 2026-06-27.** This replaces "generate ~25, score, show top 5." The deep-dive's core finding: **the bottleneck is the GATE, not the generator.** "1 excellent of 5" doesn't mean the engine can't generate good — it means *mediocre concepts survived to be shown.* No human room, studio, copywriter, or AI bats 1.000. They generate widely, kill most, rewrite the promising few, and **present only survivors.** Reliability = *industrializing the process of finding and hiding the misses*, not a generator that nails it every time.

> **The right target:** generate 50–200 internal candidates → kill most → rewrite the best → present 3–5 *finalists*, of which 3–4 are excellent. "3–4 of 5 great" applies to **presented finalists after the gauntlet**, never to raw generations. And if fewer than 3 survive, the system SAYS SO ("only 2 are strong — generate more?") — it never pads the batch to 5 with mediocrity.

## Stage 1 — Generate wide, show narrow
- Generate **50–200 internal sparks** per assignment (not 5, not 25). Use all 7 idea engines + CLoT collisions.
- Cluster into 10–15 angle families; dedupe; **kill the obvious/cliché versions** outright.
- Keep only the strongest few per cluster to advance. If you only generate five and show five, you are not operating like a pro room.

## Stage 2 — The Formal Comedy Map (every survivor must fill it)
A "technique toolkit" lists ingredients; this is the **structural map** every candidate must complete convincingly or die/rewrite. Fill, for each:
- **Core truth** · **Audience pain/desire** · **Target** (market / Zillow / process / agent — never the listing) · **Comic POV** (who's "speaking," what they're wrong about, what status they protect)
- **Audience first assumption** → **Wrong map** (what they expect) → **Reinterpretation** (the second, latent-all-along meaning) — *Greg Dean structure, see `comedy-craft/references/joke-architecture.md`*
- **Game** (the one unusual-but-true thing) + **If/then heightening ladder** (3×: "if that's true, what else is true?") — *UCB structure*
- **Act-out** (what the avatar DOES; the silence; the under-reaction; the cut) · **Callback/runner** (what returns and escalates) · **Button** (the final beat; can it recontextualize an earlier one?)
- **Visual execution** (first frame, the gag on screen — show don't tell) · **Why this is NOT the obvious version** · **Why the audience would share it**

If the map can't be filled convincingly, the concept is structurally broken. Kill or route to a rewrite.

## Stage 3 — Obviousness killer (LLMs drift to the most available joke)
Before a concept advances, force: *what are the 10 obvious jokes here? what would a mediocre creator / a generic AI / the safe LinkedIn-TikTok version write? what cliché phrases are likely? what's the audience's first thought?* — then **ban those moves.**

## Stage 4 — Failure taxonomy ("mediocre" is a symptom, not a diagnosis)
Every reject gets a *reason* label: weak truth · true-but-obvious · no game · random twist · predictable punchline · too generic · not visual · not deadpan-compatible · no escalation · weak button · too wordy · clever-but-not-funny · funny-but-not-useful-content · strong-hook-weak-payoff · wrong persona · poor production feasibility.

## Stage 5 — Targeted rewrite passes (never "make it better")
Rewrite *against the failure label*:
- No game → run UCB game extraction. · Predictable → wrong-map/misdirection pass. · Generic → specificity / language-audit pass.
- Too textual → act-out / video-execution pass. · Weak ending → callback/button pass. · Weak persuasion → copywriter pain/promise pass.
- Weak hook → hook variants (5-vector). · Weak payoff → rebuild the heightening ladder.

## Stage 6 — Pairwise taste cut (calibrate to Graeham, not to abstract theory)
Rank survivors **pairwise**, not by 1–10 score: *which of these two would you actually shoot? stronger premise? better ending? more on-voice? which would your audience share?* Log every accept/reject + the reason → over time the gate learns Graeham's real standard. Pairwise beats absolute scores for taste.

## Stage 7 — Present only survivors
Show the **top 3–5 finalists** with their filled comedy map (a judgeable mini-treatment, never a logline). **If fewer than 3 are strong, say so and offer to generate more.** Padding to a number is the exact failure that produces "1 of 5."

## The copywriter steal (persuasion-funny ≠ joke-funny)
Stand-up optimizes for laughter; an ad optimizes for *action*. A joke can be funny and still be bad copy if it distracts from the promise. The goal isn't "that was clever" — it's *"they understand my problem disturbingly well."* So steal: **voice-of-customer language** (real audience words beat generic cleverness), **pain/desire maps** (humor lands harder on a real frustration), **enemy naming** ("the productivity-industrial complex" > "being busy is annoying"), **promise discipline** (every piece knows its emotional payoff), and **specificity over cleverness** (specific recognition is often funnier than a constructed joke). *Funny is not the goal; resonant diagnosis is.*

## The honest ceiling (don't sell the dream)
- 100% raw quality: impossible. · 100% finalist quality: unrealistic. · Consistently competent: achievable.
- 3/5 finalists excellent after heavy filtering: plausible. · 4/5: possible in a narrow format with strong taste calibration, not guaranteed. · Every finished video hits with the audience: impossible.

The buildable dream: **a brutal AI-assisted writers' room that out-ranges a human, applies formal comedy structure, learns the taste-holder's standard, rewrites by diagnosis, kills aggressively, and packages only survivors for excellent video execution.** That's real, and it's how the pros actually win.
