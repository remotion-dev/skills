# Identity Mirror — 5-Year Self-Audit

**Category:** Personal Custom Prompt
**Added:** 2026-05-31
**Source:** Personal — saved by Graeham Watts
**Curated by:** Graeham Watts

## What it does

A four-prompt sequence you run inside a single ChatGPT chat that already has long memory of you. It turns the model into a mirror: it builds a complete profile of who you are, names the contradictions between your words and actions, projects the exact version of you in five years if nothing changes, then flips it to show the five-year version if you fix your single biggest blind spot starting today. The power comes from running all four in order in the same thread — each answer feeds the next.

## When to use it

- Annual or quarterly self-review / goal reset
- When you feel stuck and can't name why
- Before a big life or career decision
- When you want an outside read on your patterns without paying a coach
- New Year / birthday reflection

## How to run it

Open a **new chat in ChatGPT** (use an account with memory turned on and real history, or it has nothing to mirror). Paste the prompts **one at a time, in order, in the same chat.** Let each answer land before sending the next.

## The prompts

**Prompt 1 — Profile**

> Using only things I've actually said or done across our conversations, build a profile of me: communication style, values, what I'm insecure about, what I'm working toward. Cite the specific behavior behind each claim. Tag every claim as fact / strong inference / weak guess. Where you have no data for a section, say so instead of filling it in. No flattery — if the evidence is unflattering, say it plainly.

**Prompt 2 — Contradictions**

> Where do my words not match my actions? Give me the specific quote or behavior on each side of the gap. Rank them by how much each is actually costing me, and separate real contradictions from things that are just normal human tension. End with the one that matters most and why.

**Prompt 3 — Dark projection**

> If I keep operating exactly as I do now, give me the most likely version of me in five years — not the worst case — across career, relationships, health, mindset. Ground each prediction in a current behavior. Flag which domains you have real data on and which are pattern-inference. Give me your honest odds, not a dramatized story.

**Prompt 4 — Fixed projection**

> Name my single highest-leverage blind spot and justify why that one over the others. Then map the five-year fixed version with dated milestones — including what the fix costs me and what would make it fail. Keep the same evidence and confidence discipline.

## Note

Prompt 3 can hit hard — people often find the "if nothing changes" projection uncomfortable to read. That discomfort is the point; Prompt 4 is what turns it into a plan. If you're going through a rough stretch, run Prompt 4 right after 3 so you don't sit in the negative version.

## Why it works

The model isn't psychic — it's pattern-matching your own words back at you across hundreds of conversations, stripped of your self-flattery. Prompts 1–2 establish a shared factual base; Prompt 3 extrapolates the current trajectory; Prompt 4 changes one variable and re-extrapolates. It's a cheap, fast version of the gap analysis a good coach would walk you through.
