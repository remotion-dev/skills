New skill set up for you — switchy-engine. From now on you don't make QR codes by hand. You build the postcard, hand it to Claude in Cowork, and it generates a tracked QR (so we can see scans and retarget people who scan it), names and files it correctly in Switchy, drops it on our dashboard, and syncs it to GitHub.

Install (one time)

Open the attached switchy-qr.skill file and click "Save skill" in Cowork. (It's also in our GitHub repo: Graehamwatts/skills → skills/switchy-qr if you'd rather pull it.) This is the lightweight, QR-only version — all you need to make postcard QR codes.

How to generate a QR (every postcard)

  1. Finish the postcard in Canva.
  2. In Cowork, say: "Generate a QR code for this postcard" and upload the postcard PDF.
  3. Claude takes control of Chrome and opens Switchy. You log in once when it asks (it can't type your password for you — see login below).
  4. Claude then does the rest automatically: creates the tracked link with the right landing page + UTM + our Meta/Google pixels, files it in the "Post card qr" folder, names it clearly, opens the QR designer, and downloads the QR PNG for you.
  5. You drop that QR into the Canva postcard and export for print.
  6. Claude adds the link to our Switchy dashboard and syncs it to GitHub.
